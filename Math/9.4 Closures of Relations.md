#### Reflexive
$$
\forall a \in A : aRa
$$
#### Symmetric
$$
\forall a,b \in A : (aRb \Rightarrow bRa)
$$
#### Antisymmetric
$$
\forall a,b \in A : (aRb \land a \neq b \Rightarrow b \not Ra)
$$
#### Transitive
$$
\forall a,b,c \in A : (aRb \land bRc \Rightarrow aRc)
$$

R = \{(1,3),(2,1),(2,2),(2,3),(3,1),(3,3)\} \newline \space \text{relation on} \space\{1,2,3,4\}

$$
r(R) = R \cup \{(1,1),(4,4)\} = R = {(1,1),(1,3),(2,1),(2,2),(2,3),(3,1),(3,3) (4,4)}

# Symmetric Closures
### example
$$
R=\{(1,3),(2,1),(2,3),(3,1),(3,3)\}
$$
	0 0 1
	1 0 1
	1 0 1
The symmetric closure of R is
$$
S(R) = \{(1,3),(2,1),(2,3),(3,1),(3,3),(1,2),(3,2)\}
$$

	0 1 1
	1 0 1
	1 1 1

The symmetric closure of a relation R is 
$$
S(R)= R \cup \{(a,b)|(b,a) \in R \}
$$
### example
$$
S(R_{<})= R_{<} \cup R_{>} = R_{\neq}
$$
$$
S(\text{"Same parity"}) = \text{"Same parity"}
$$

# Transitive Closures
### example
$$
R=\{(1,2),(2,3),(3,4)\}
$$
The transitive closure of R is 
$$
t(R)=\{(1,2),(2,3),(3,4),(2,4),(1,3),(2,4),(1,4)\}
$$
$$
t(R)=\{(c,a)| \exists b :  aR^{*}b \land bR^{*}c\} \cup R
$$
$$
= \{(a,c)|a \rightarrow c \space \text{(En vej fra a til c)} \space i \space G_{R} \}
$$
The transitive closure of any relation R is
$$
t(R)=R^{*}
$$

For at den er transitive closure, mangler der bare (2,3) og (1,1), da man kan gå fra 2 til 3, så laver vi en genvej der. og man kan gå fra 1 til 3 og fra 3 til 1, sp derfor et loop som genvej.
$$
t(R)=R \cup R^{2} \cup R^{3}
