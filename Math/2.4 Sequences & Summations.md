## Product Notation (∏)

**Product notation**, denoted by the symbol ∏ (capital pi), represents the multiplication of a sequence of terms.

**Definition**: The product $∏_{k=a}^b f(k)$ means to multiply together the values of the expression f(k) for k taking all integer values from a to b.

**Notation**: $∏_{k=a}^b f(k) = f(a) × f(a+1) × f(a+2) × ⋯ × f(b)$

This is analogous to summation notation ∑, where ∑ adds terms together and ∏ multiplies terms together.

**Example**: Consider the product $∏_{k=1}^4$ k: This means: k=1 × k=2 × k=3 × k=4 = 1 × 2 × 3 × 4 = 24

**Key Distinction**:

- Σ (sigma) represents addition: $∑_{k=1}^n k = 1 + 2 + 3 + ⋯ + n$
- ∏ (pi) represents multiplication: $∏_{k=1}^n k = 1 × 2 × 3 × ⋯ × n$

**Factorial Notation**: The factorial n! can be expressed using product notation as: $n! = ∏_{k=1}^n (n - k + 1)$

**Verification of Factorial Expression**: For $n = 5: ∏_{k=1}^5 (5 - k + 1) = (5-1+1) × (5-2+1) × (5-3+1) × (5-4+1) × (5-5+1) = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 5! = 120$
**Comparison**:

| Operation | Notation         | Meaning                  |
| --------- | ---------------- | ------------------------ |
| Summation | $∑_{k=a}^b f(k)$ | f(a) + f(a+1) + ⋯ + f(b) |
| Product   | $∏_{k=a}^b f(k)$ | f(a) × f(a+1) × ⋯ × f(b) |
