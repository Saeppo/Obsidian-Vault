# Divisibility
**Divisibility definition:**

- a | b means: there exists an integer k such that b=a⋅k
    

Now, check your examples:

1. 3 | 6
    
    - Can we find an integer k such that 6=3⋅k?
        
    - Yes, k=2k = 2k=2. True.
        
2. 6 | 3
    
    - Can we find an integer k such that 3=6⋅k?
        
    - No, because k=0.5 is **not an integer**. False.
        

**Rule of thumb:** the number on the **left** must “fit evenly” into the number on the **right**. It doesn’t matter which is bigger; what matters is whether the **right-hand number is a multiple of the left-hand number**.
$$
\text{for any } a,b,c \in Z, a \neq 0
$$
## Theorem 4.1.1
(i)
$$
a|b \land a|c \implies a|(b+c)
$$
(ii)
$$
a|b \implies \forall k \in Z: a|kb
$$
(iii)
$$
a|b \land b|c \implies a|c
$$
## Corollary
$$
a|b \land a|c \implies \forall k,l \in Z : a|(kb+lc)
$$

# Congruence
$$

$$
\underline{a \equiv b \text{(mod m)}} \Leftrightarrow m|(a-b)
$$
a is congruent to b modulo m
Det underline stykke er for biimplies hedder "Congruence"

**Modulo with Negative Numbers (Quick Rule)**  
To compute **–a mod n**, first reduce **a mod n**, then correct the sign:

1. Compute **r = a mod n**.
    
2. Replace **–a mod n** with **(n – r) mod n**.
    
3. Result is always in **0 … n–1**.
Example:  
–17 mod 7 → 17 mod 7 = 3 → 7 – 3 = **4**.  
–66 mod 7 → 66 mod 7 = 3 → 7 – 3 = **4**.
